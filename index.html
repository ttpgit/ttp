Our Story

Life is very strange. You choose to answer a call, and that leads you to a job, where you meet people who become your friends and through them you migrate to different countries. I was young. I had visions and dreams for a happy life.

But then, BANG! The pandemic happened. It wasn't in any part of my calculations when I planned my life. Like many of us, I failed financially. I lost control, my savings were gone, and I was left with a mountain of debt—around $100,000.

The weight of it was too much. I was recently diagnosed with severe depression after I reached a point where I almost didn't make it. But I reached for help, and I am still here. I share this because I want to build this project with truth. I need your help, and if I can raise my foot again with your support, I'll spend every cent I get from this community to help the next person.

To those of you who feel the same—who lost everything and feel alone—trust me. We are sinking, but if you reach for my hand, I promise: the moment I find solid ground, I turn around for you. We don't wait for systems to save us; we save each other.

Even the smallest amount can help me and add you to the queue. Imagine if only half of the people who struggle with debt supported this program with a small amount, maybe the cost of a coffee — me and many others could become debt-free and get a second chance.

The TTP Architecture

01

The Receiving Hub

The addresses listed on this page are the permanent entry points for the TTP Protocol. All community support is gathered here under public scrutiny.

02

Bridging the Current Gap

Incoming support is first applied to the current person in the queue. Every transaction is visible on the blockchain, creating an immutable ledger of our progress.

03

Automatic FIFO Flow

The moment the current bridge is filled, the protocol activates the next person. All remaining and upcoming funds are immediately transferred to the next person in the queue based on who joined first.

04

Queue Priority

Your place in the future queue is determined by the timestamp of your first contribution. Those who help build the bridge are the first to be carried across it when they need it.
